Series

  • Wave two: evidence visibility, completion cohorts, condition families, and sponsor concentration.
  • Wave three: rule eras, a PubMed publication audit, and disease-specific oncology, cardiovascular, and metabolic projects.
  • Wave four: enrollment size, geography scale, design purpose, completion delay, and trial architecture.
  • Wave five: intervention family, named-country, stopped-trial, outcome-density, and actual-field audits.
  • Wave six: U.S.-global buckets, modality sponsor repeaters, country-condition cells, disease geography, and condition-sponsor repeaters.
  • Wave seven: country-sponsor repeaters, U.S.-presence sponsor classes, and industry-family repeaters.
  • Wave eight: risk-adjusted hiddenness, overdue results clocks, publication indexing gaps, and probable ACT/FDAAA debt.
  • Wave nine: sponsor, country, and condition watchlists plus black-box trials and strict-proxy repeaters.
  • Wave ten: sponsor ghosts, country ghosts, condition ghosts, black-box sponsor repeaters, and strict-core black-box audits.
  • Wave eleven: sponsor debt, country debt, condition debt, narrative-gap repeaters, and actual-discipline repeaters.
  • Wave twelve: sponsor, country, and condition ancient backlog plus description-black-box and completion-timing repeaters.
  • Wave thirteen: country and condition description black-box projects plus sponsor, country, and condition enrollment-gap projects.
  • The hub is the visible front door to all sixty public CT.gov story pages.
